Frequently Asked Questions

The Common University Entrance Test (CUET) is an entrance exam for admissions to undergraduate courses offered by the Central Universities including Delhi University, BHU, Jamia Milia Islamia, JNU, AMU, University of Allahabad, the University of Hyderabad among others. The exam will test one’s language aptitude, subject knowledge, and general mental ability.

CUET scores will be used for admissions to undergraduate courses of 45 Central Universities including Delhi University, BHU, Jamia Milia Islamia, JNU, AMU, University of Allahabad, the University of Hyderabad among others. TISS and seven other institutions have also shown their eagerness to accept CUET Scores for admissions to their undergraduate courses.

As per basic CUET eligibility criteria, the candidate who passed the 10+2 or its equivalent exam from a recognized board can apply. Minimum of 50% marks in class 12th for General category candidates and 45% marks for SC/ST candidates. Subjects required are Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics, And/ OR Biology depending upon the course you are looking for.

CUET is designed to evaluate one’s understanding of language, knowledge of subjects studied in class XII, and general reasoning and quantitative skills. The paper will comprise three sections: Language, Subject Domain Test, and General Aptitude Test.

CUET is expected to be conducted in two slots with each slot having a time duration of four hours.

Each institute will release its own CUET 2023 cut-off list for admission into various courses. Candidates have to qualify for the minimum cut-off criteria to get admission.
